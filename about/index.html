So, I'm John Randazzo.

I'm a senior at UCSB majoring in Financial Mathematics and Statistics. A fault of mine is my intellectual promiscuity. Some of my interests are probability, mathematical finance, quantitative ecology, biostatistics (especially survival analysis!), and social dynamics.

My current research is at the intersection of machine learning and survival analysis, where I test different approaches to modeling time-varying covariates for Cox Proportional Hazards and Joint Models in high-dimensional/messy datasets. Although the data I currently use are confidential, my hope is to publish the functions I write to CRAN and have my very own R package as a contribution to general knowledge.
