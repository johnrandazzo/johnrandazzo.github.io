So, I'm John Randazzo.

I'm a 21-year-old senior at UCSB majoring in Financial Mathematics and Statistics. A fault of mine is my intellectual promiscuity. Some of my interests are probability, mathematical finance, quantitative ecology, biostatistics (especially survival analysis!), and social dynamics.

My current research is at the intersection of machine learning and survival analysis, where I test different approaches to modeling time-varying covariates for Cox Proportional Hazards and Joint Models in high-dimensional/high-noise settings. Although the data I currently use are confidential, I plan to publish the functions I write to comprise my first-ever R package on CRAN. If you *really* want to know more, contact me!

In my free time I enjoy being outside, swimming, lifting weights, and all forms of music. My favorite place in Santa Barbara is the roof of my house, and my spirit animal is a Sonoran Desert Toad.
