So, I'm John Randazzo.

I'm a 21-year-old senior at UCSB majoring in Financial Mathematics and Statistics. Some of my interests are computer graphics, probability, mathematical finance, information security, quantitative ecology, and biostatistics (especially survival analysis!).

This year, I am taking fewer classes than in the past--I currently work as a data team intern at UCSB's National Center for Ecological Analysis and Synthesis (NCEAS) and as a course reader (yup, just a homework grader) for PSTAT 160A, an upper division probability course.

My current research is at the intersection of machine learning and survival analysis, where I test different approaches to modeling time-varying covariates for Cox Proportional Hazards and Joint Models in high-dimensional/high-noise settings. My advisors are Professors Ian Duncan and Jiyoun Myung. The data I currently use are confidential, but you wouldn't want them anyways. I also like to make simulations using Blender and am (technically) a professional web scraper. I also don't bite.

Upon finally being dragged outside, I tend to enjoy swimming, hiking, and lifting weights. I am tremendously lucky to live within skipping distance to the beach, and my spirit animal is a Sonoran Desert Toad.
